Rules & Regulations

DPSC Sand Volleyball Tournament · Spring 2026

👥

1. Team Composition & Player Requirements

1.1
Mandatory Strength: A team must always play at full strength even if the number of players is less on the opponent's side. (Ex: If Team A has 5 players and Team B has 7, Team B must play with 7 players at all times).
1.2
Minimum Requirements: A minimum of 5 players is required for the game to start and proceed.
1.3
Forfeiture: If 5 players are not present at the start time, a 15-minute courtesy wait is given, after which the game is forfeited to the opponents.
1.4
Injury Protocol: If a player is injured, a 5-minute timeout is provided for recovery. If the player has not recovered, they must sit out for the current set to allow the game to continue.
🔄

2. Rotation & Positioning

2.1
Mandatory Rotation: All seven players must rotate mandatorily.
2.2
Rotation Timing: Rotation is mandatory whenever a point is won and the serving turn comes back to the team.
2.3
Positioning Maintenance: Positioning should be maintained throughout the game. At any point if a position is swapped illegally, a point is awarded to the opponent team.
2.4
Legal Swapping: Swapping of positions is allowed only from the first row and only between adjacent positions (Left Spiker/Setter or Setter/Right Spiker).
2.5
Front Row Restrictions: No 3-position swaps are allowed from the front row.
2.6
Back Row Restrictions: No swapping is allowed from the back row.
2.7
Swap Execution: The swap should happen by holding hands and should occur only after the serve is completed.
🏐

3. Back Row & Net Violations

3.1
Back Row Spiking: Spiking is allowed from the back row as long as the jump starts from a back-row position. A guideline will be placed outside the court for back-row guidance (Umpires to judge).
3.2
Back Row Foul: If a back-row player comes to the front row and spikes, it's a foul and a point is awarded to the opponent team.
3.3
Net Touch: Net touch is not allowed at any point during active play. Touching the net is a foul and a point will be awarded to the opponent team.
3.4
Crossing Under the Net: Spikers or offensive line players should not cross a whole foot under the net. As long as 1% of a foot remains on the line/original side, it is valid. If a single whole foot or both feet cross the line, it is a foul. (Umpires' discretion — intention is to avoid injuries).
3.5
Reaching Over the Net: Reaching over the net while the ball is live in the opponent's court (Pass 1, Pass 2) is considered a Reach Over Foul — a point is awarded to the opponent.
3.6
Reach Over Exception: The only exception to Rule 3.5 is when the third player plays the final pass or when the opponent team is trying to block a spike.
3.7
Boundary Lines: Line ropes will be utilized in both courts. Anytime the line is touched or the rope moves on ball impact, the ball is considered "In."
✋

4. Ball Handling & Gameplay

4.1
Block Touches: A ball is considered blocked for a second touch to be continued by the same player only if, at the time of the block, both the spiker and blocker were in contact with the ball simultaneously.
4.2
Carry Foul: Whenever a player has two open palms for lifting the ball, it's considered a carry foul and a point is awarded to the opponent team.
4.3
Rollover Foul: Whenever a ball has two impacts anywhere on the body, it's considered a rollover foul and a point is awarded to the opponent team.
4.4
Legal Touches: A single touch anywhere on the shoulder, head, hand, or a kick is considered legal.
🔔

5. Serving & Timing

5.1
Service Whistle: A server must wait until the referee's whistle to serve. If served before the whistle, it's a foul and a point is awarded to the opponent team.
5.2
Service Line Warning: Touching the line while serving results in a first warning (once per team, per game — not per set). The next violation results in a foul and a point awarded to the opponent team.
5.3
Timeouts: Each side is allowed to take one timeout per set for a maximum of 45 seconds.
5.4
Side Switch — 21-Point Game: In a game played to 21 points, teams switch sides when either team first reaches 11 points. Whichever team reaches 11 first triggers the side change.
5.5
Side Switch — 18-Point Game: In a game played to 18 points, teams switch sides when either team first reaches 9 points. Whichever team reaches 9 first triggers the side change.
⚖️

6. Officiating & Conduct

6.1
Continuous Play: Play should continue until the referee calls to stop, even if violations are noted by the opponent. Interrupting the game results in a point for the opponent.
6.2
Referee Authority: Referee decision is final. In cases where the referee cannot resolve line calls or disputes after consulting umpires and peers, the point will be replayed with a re-serve.
6.3
Captain's Role: Captains should honor the referee's decision and keep playing.
6.4
Dispute Communication: Only captains are allowed to talk to referees regarding disputes. If a team member talks to referees directly, one warning per game is given; subsequently, a point is forfeited and awarded to the opponent.
6.5
Exceptional Scenarios: If any exceptional scenario not mentioned above occurs, referees will consult the organizing committee to decide on actions and potential rule amendments.
